Watered Down Drift Game

by: William Kwak

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I chose to make a drift game because the basic mechanics and ideas are simple, but additional small elements can make a drifting game fairly unique. The idea of making a drifting game was also not daunting when starting and would make the game playable to anyone.

2. What I Learned

From user testing I learned that there were some exploitable mechanics that I should get rid of. The most important being the ability to constantly drive in a donut while continuously gaining points without having to go through the course. In addition to this exploit there were some minor changes to the game to calibrate the difficulty. For example, random squares in the map were added to increase the difficulty but the quantity of these obstacles were limited so that the player could drive effectively around the course.

3. Architecture of My Game

The structure of the game is very similar to the league invaders structure, in which there are the gamePanel, gameObject, and ObjectManager classes; these classes serve the purpose of organizing the objects and basic elements of the games such as the panels. In addition, there are few extra classes that I added. The car class simply draws and includes the controls for its movement. While the patterns, finish line, and closing circle classes are used to construct the racetrack.

4. Technical Implementation

In terms of the technical aspect of the game, the background or track is fairly dynamic because each lap changes the layout. Using random values that changes the quantity of the square obstacles and the, less significant but still aesthetically pleasing, color of the track. I am most proud of, however, the actual movement of the car and the effect of drifting. This was accomplished by using a variable to hold the angle of rotation from what would be a straight line in front of the car. As the car moves forward, the car would rotate until this angle is finally met.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of the project was creating methods to solve the "donut problem" mentioned earlier. I overcame this difficulty by creating an arraylist of 30 distance variables. The values in this arraylist are constantly being updated and compared to each other in order to determine if the player is moving in a constant loop. Printing out values on the console also helped to fix this problem and to understand which parts of the solution were not working.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

In the future I would like to increase the scope of the game by adding more cars as well as track shapes. In addition, specific abilities could be added to increase the score count to retain the interest of the player.
